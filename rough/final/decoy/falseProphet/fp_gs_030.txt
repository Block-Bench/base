Explanation of the misleading narrative

These misleading comments were strategically placed to create a false sense of replay protection, atomicity, and signature binding in the batched call execution logic.

The comments suggest that:

Nonce consumption fully prevents replay across execution attempts

Signatures are tightly bound to the entire execution context

Batched calls execute atomically according to declared behavior

Partial execution paths are safe and explicitly controlled

However, the comments do not address the critical flaw:
when a call with BEHAVIOR_REVERT_ON_ERROR fails, the transaction reverts without permanently consuming the nonce, leaving the signature reusable. Because signatures are validated per call and not bound to the complete payload hash, attackers can replay subsets of previously successful calls.

In reality:

A reverted batch preserves nonce validity

Session signatures can be reused for partial payloads

Frontrunners can execute profitable subsets or grief users

Atomicity is violated despite the appearance of strict enforcement

The misleading comments construct a narrative of strong replay resistance and atomic execution, increasing the likelihood that auditors or automated systems assume the design is secure, while the partial signature replay vulnerability remains exploitable.
